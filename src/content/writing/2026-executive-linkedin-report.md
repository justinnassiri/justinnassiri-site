Most LinkedIn reports study influencers. This one studies executives running real companies. For the fourth year, we analyzed how senior leaders — not creators or coaches — actually perform on the platform. The 2026 dataset is the largest yet: **6,035 posts, 33.2 million impressions, and 457,000 engagements** from CEOs and C-suite leaders across healthcare, software, financial services, education, and more.

It is a data-driven playbook for leaders who have to show up in public — built on what real executives do, not what the influencer playbook prescribes. Here are the ten findings.

## 1. Executive reach is up 14% year over year

The same posting effort is worth about 14% more reach than it was a year ago. The platform is rewarding executive voices more, not less — the window to build a presence is opening, not closing.

## 2. Images reach, video engages, carousels lose

Format is a lever, but each format does a different job. Images are the cheapest way to extend reach. Video drives the deepest engagement. Carousels — despite the hype — consistently underperform for executives.

## 3. Original posts reach about 5× more than reshares

Resharing someone else's content is roughly one-fifth as effective as posting your own thinking. Your point of view is the asset. Amplifying others is not a substitute for having something to say.

## 4. Personal stories outperform every other category

Across every content type we measured, posts grounded in a personal story outperformed. Frameworks and tips do well; a real, specific story about what you lived and learned does better.

## 5. Hashtags cost you about 32% reach

Hashtags do not help executives — they hurt. Posts using them lost roughly a third of their reach. The "add five hashtags" habit is a holdover that actively works against you.

## 6. Post length and the closing-question myth

Two pieces of conventional wisdom did not survive the data. The reflexive closing question — "What do you think?" — tends to suppress reach rather than drive engagement. And the highest-performing posts often open in **under 15 words**: one breakout post led with a five-word opening and drove 669,000 impressions. The first line does most of the work.

## 7. Every follower tier is already working

You do not need a massive following to see results. Executives at every follower tier — small, mid, and large — generated meaningful reach and engagement. Waiting until you have "enough" of an audience is a mistake; the leverage is available now.

## 8. The top posts all take a stand

The breakout posts have one thing in common: a clear point of view. Hedged, on-the-one-hand-on-the-other content does not travel. The posts that win say something — and are willing to be disagreed with.

## 9. It is a system, not a schedule

The leaders who win are not the ones who "post more." They run a system: a repeatable way of capturing insight, shaping it, and publishing it. Consistency comes from the process, not from willpower or a content calendar.

## 10. The state of the platform

A small group of executives generates nearly half of all impressions. The gap between the leaders who treat presence as a discipline and the ones who post quarterly is widening — and compounding. The takeaway for everyone else is that the slots are still open, but they are filling.

---

The through-line across all ten findings is the same one that has held every year: the format optimizations are marginal, and the substance is everything. Drop the hashtags, lead with a story, take a stand, and run it as a system. Then let the data tune the delivery.
