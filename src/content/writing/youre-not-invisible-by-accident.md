Nearly every CEO I talk to says some version of the same thing: "I don't like social media. I don't want the spotlight. I don't have an ego." I believe them. But their invisibility is not humility — it is a decision. And it is costing their organization more than they realize.

## "Let the work speak for itself" stops working at scale

Early on, great work does generate recognition on its own. A small company, a tight network, and visible results are enough. But somewhere around 50 to 100 employees, that equation breaks. The organization is now bigger than the room of people who can see the work directly. Investors, customers, and prospective hires are forming opinions about your company without ever seeing what you actually do. At that scale, "the work speaks for itself" quietly becomes "no one outside the building knows how good we are."

## Humility or hiding?

There is a real difference between humility and hiding, and it is worth being honest with yourself about which one you are doing. Humility is doing excellent work and not needing credit for it. Hiding is staying invisible because being seen is uncomfortable — and then calling that discomfort a virtue. One serves the mission. The other quietly starves it of the visibility it needs to recruit, sell, and raise.

## The submarine on the bridge

I learned leadership on a submarine, and the model fits. A junior officer keeps their head down and executes their watch. A captain has to be on the bridge — visible, setting direction, owning the picture for everyone else. Growth-stage CEOs face the same identity shift: from heads-down operator to visible leader. You cannot run the company from the engine room anymore. Your team, your investors, and your customers all need you where they can see you.

## The reframe

If you do not have an ego about it, good — that probably means you will be a more trusted, less performative voice than most. But understand what your silence actually is. It is not modesty. It is a choice with a cost, made on behalf of an organization that needs you visible. You are not invisible by accident. The question is whether you will choose differently.
